The Science Behind WiFi Security

Pulse Guard leverages a principle from wireless communications research: WiFi signals are affected by everything in their environment, including people. When a person enters a room, their body absorbs and reflects radio waves, creating measurable disturbances in the WiFi signal pattern that your MacBook's adapter can detect.

Channel State Information (CSI) Analysis

Traditional motion detection relies on cameras or infrared sensors. Pulse Guard takes a fundamentally different approach by analyzing WiFi Channel State Information — the fine-grained signal data that describes how radio waves travel between your router and your Mac. CSI captures amplitude and phase information across multiple subcarriers, providing a detailed fingerprint of the wireless environment. When this fingerprint changes, Pulse Guard knows something has moved.

Calibration & Baseline Learning

During initial setup, Pulse Guard learns the baseline signal pattern of your room when it is empty. This calibration phase typically takes 2-3 minutes. Once calibrated, the app continuously compares live signal data against this baseline, detecting even subtle environmental changes. The sensitivity threshold is fully adjustable — you can fine-tune it to ignore pets while still detecting human-sized movement.

Real-World Use Cases

Pulse Guard is ideal for home offices, apartments, and small spaces where camera surveillance feels invasive. Remote workers use it to know when someone enters their workspace. Parents use it as a lightweight baby room monitor. Privacy-conscious users appreciate that no visual data is ever captured or stored — just radio wave patterns that cannot identify individuals.
