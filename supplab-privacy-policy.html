Privacy Policy

SuppLab

Effective Date: July 19, 2026
Developer: Umay (indie developer)
Bundle ID: dev.umay.supplab
Website: https://umay.dev

Your privacy is fundamental to how SuppLab is built. SuppLab (the “App”) has no backend servers and no user accounts. Everything you create in the App — your supplement stack, experiments, check-ins, logs, reminders, and any health data — stays entirely on your device. This Privacy Policy explains what that means in practice.

In short: We do not collect, transmit, sell, or share your personal or health data. There are no analytics SDKs, no advertising, and no trackers. Your data never leaves your iPhone unless you explicitly export or share it yourself.

1. Information We Collect

The Developer does not collect or receive any personal information from you. The App does not require an account, a name, an email address, or a login.

The following information is created and stored locally on your device only, and is never sent to us:

  • App data you enter: supplements in your stack, experiments, daily check-ins, intake logs, reminder settings, and optional cost figures. This is stored on-device using Apple’s on-device database (SwiftData).
  • Apple Health (HealthKit) data: if you grant permission, the App reads read-only health metrics — sleep analysis, heart rate variability (HRV), resting heart rate, and step count — to build your personal baseline and measure supplement effects. This data is processed on your device and is never uploaded, and the App does not write any data back to Apple Health.
  • Camera (label scanning): if you use the label scanner, the camera image is analyzed on your device (using Apple’s Vision framework) to recognize supplement ingredient names. Images are processed transiently in memory and are not stored, saved, or uploaded.

2. How We Use Information

Because your data stays on your device, it is used only by the App, on your device, to provide its features:

  • To run your randomized personal (N-of-1) experiments and generate your decision cards.
  • To build your baseline and analytics from Apple Health data and your check-ins.
  • To deliver local notifications (protocol and stack reminders) scheduled on your device.
  • To match scanned ingredients to the in-app library.

HealthKit-specific commitment: Health data obtained through HealthKit is used solely to provide the App’s features to you. We do not, and will never, use HealthKit data for advertising, marketing, or data-mining, and we do not sell it or disclose it to any third party.

3. Third Parties

SuppLab does not include third-party analytics, advertising networks, or tracking SDKs. The only third party involved is Apple:

  • Purchases: subscriptions and in-app purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store and StoreKit. We never see or receive your payment card details. Apple’s handling of your purchase information is governed by Apple’s Privacy Policy.
  • Apple Health: health data is provided locally by Apple’s HealthKit framework, with your permission, and remains on your device.

4. Data Retention and Deletion

Since we hold no copy of your data, there is nothing for us to retain or delete on a server. You are always in control:

  • You can delete all app data at any time from Settings → Delete All Data inside the App.
  • Deleting (uninstalling) the App removes all of its on-device data from your device.
  • You can revoke the App’s access to Apple Health at any time in the iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Health.

5. Your Rights

Applicable privacy laws (such as the GDPR and CCPA) give you rights over your personal data, including the rights to access, correct, delete, and port it. Because the App collects no personal data and stores everything locally under your control, you can exercise these rights directly: all of your data is on your device, viewable in the App, and deletable by you at any time. If you have any questions about your rights, contact us using the details in Section 8.

6. Children’s Privacy

SuppLab is intended for generally healthy adults and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect information from children under the age of 13 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). The App should not be used by anyone under 18 without the involvement of a parent, guardian, and a healthcare professional.

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will always be posted at this URL with an updated effective date. Material changes will be reflected here; your continued use of the App after an update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

8. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or your data, please reach us via https://umay.dev.
